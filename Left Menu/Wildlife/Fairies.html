Fairies, soulless beings neither flora nor fauna, share many properties with demons: they are invisible, and only felt, not seen. Typically they manifest as derangements of the soul, potentially giving rise to aberrations, though normally the being they parasite suffers at most negligible damage to their expressive capacity. Instead, the primary danger of the fairy is the damage it inflicts on the mental domain of the soul. The Mtera of the fairy, as far as it is understood, is to cause delusions relating to a failure to correctly recognize, delineate, and/or identify oneself or others. In plain terms, the fairy is a creature borne of madness.

FÉE INUSITÉE
The first fairy to be described, by Zhamayne scholars. Literally translated as "unusual fairy," la fée inusitée was originally meant to class every fairy as one, but its definition when applied to a single species has been restricted as a more varied collection was revealed. Some texts still use the name as a catch-all instead of "fairy," however.
