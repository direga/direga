SILKRIBBON BUTTERFLIES
These multicolored creatures have wings the size of plates, at the bottoms of which are affixed meter-long streamers. They can be seen in great volumes over stiffgrass fields, where enormous jadefire irises require their pollination. Very rarely do apoke complete the process to become a butterfly, but when one does, it is often a silkribbon. The silk from such a butterfly is prized more than the silk from immature apoke-threading techniques, for "lesser" silk aim to mimic the elegance of the natural weave, an ultimately fruitless endeavor.
